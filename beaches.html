Beaches to visit...

Anaxos

Anaxos is a nice tourist resort lying 60 km north west of Mytilene, the capital of Lesvos between the villages Petra and Molyvos. The area combines an extraordinary natural beauty and some modern facilities that make this area ideal for family and young couple holidays. The beach is surrounded by olive groves, oak-trees and pine forests. Since 2011, local government reform made it part of the municipality of Lesbos. Anaxos coastline combines exceptional natural beauties, with a dark blue sea.


Molyvos

Molyvos beach is full with small stones but once you get out a few feet this gives way to soft sand and there are few beaches that have the view you get when you turn around and face the land. It is a view of the town and the castle, so beautiful that you will risk the possible destruction of your camera in order to get a photo. There are a few cafe-bars and restaurants in this part of the village. The Delfini beach is a nice one to try out as it is considered one of the best on the island.


Petra

Petra has the largest most organized beach of the island of Lesbos, which lies in 57k north of Mytilene. It offers various facilities like umbrellas, sun beds, showers and changing cabins. Some restaurants and taverns are found in the close distance of the beach. It is by far the most touristy beach of the island. If you are looking for privacy and to relax, then Petra beach shouldn't be on your priority list. However, if you are young and looking for the most fun beach parties then you have come to the right place.



Route access

Anaxos, Molyvos and Petra are easily accessible by public transport busses that run throughout the day until 12 at night, check the map for more route details on these beaches.
