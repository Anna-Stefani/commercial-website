History


Mithymna is a town and former municipality of Lesbos. Before 1919 its official name was Molyvos - that name dates back to
the end of the Byzantine Era, but is still in a common use today. The second most important town of the island, it is known for its beautiful castle.
The castle was built around 1373, during the Byzantine period, on the site of the acropolis of the ancient city, after the island of Lesbos
was granted to Francesco Gattilusio.

Petra takes its name from the giant monolith located some distance inland and capped by the 18th century church of Panagia Glykofiloussa.
The area is extending to 72km and with a population of 3,358 it's the largest town of the area. It is a traditional town with sandy beaches, old stone houses and Levantine-style balconies overhanging the streets.

Mytilene is a city founded in the 11th century BC. Mytilene is the capital and port of the island of Lesbos and also the capital of the North Aegean Region.
The seat of the governor of the North Aegean Region is also situated in Mytilene. Additionally, it is also one of 13 municipalities (counties) on the island of Lesbos.
The Leadership of the island in the 7th century BC concentrated in the eastern hinterland of Mytilene. Her most famous citizens were poets like Sappho and
Alcaeus and the statesmen of Pittacus.

Plomari previously named Megalochori, was the biggest rural centre of the region, and was constituted by 18 small rural villages.
In 1841-1843 big fires burned down old Plomari, while few years later, in 1850, the big frost destroyed the olives of Lesbos and led to desperation the
residents of Plomari. The town had great economic growth from the end of the 19th century up to the second World War. The manufacture of harbour gave new
impulse in trade and transportation also developed in the 1950s.
